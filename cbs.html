About Color Blindness

Color blindness affects approximately 1 in 12 men and 1 in 200 women worldwide. This tool helps simulate how people with different types of color vision deficiencies perceive images.

Deuteranopia

Difficulty distinguishing between red and green colors. Most common type.

Protanopia

Reduced sensitivity to red light, making reds appear darker.

Tritanopia

Difficulty distinguishing between blue and yellow colors. Rare condition.

Achromatopsia

Complete color blindness, seeing only in shades of gray. Very rare.
